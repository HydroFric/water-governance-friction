About HydroFric

📌 Purpose & Scope – For rural water supply

The mission of HydroFric is to help rural water service providers and community members diagnose the most urgent gaps between policy promises, available resources, on‑ground infrastructure, and what families actually experience at the tap. The tool is suitable for any rural setting – from a single water point to a small piped scheme serving several villages – and can be used by village water committees, district water officers, NGO field staff, local government planners, and community health workers.

🧠 Conceptual Framework & Methodology

HydroFric is grounded in the Socio‑Technical Friction (STF) Framework, which builds on internationally recognised water governance principles, including the OECD Principles on Water Governance (2015), the SADC Revised Protocol on Shared Watercourses (2000), Integrated Water Resources Management (IWRM) as promoted by the Global Water Partnership, and rural water access indicators from UN‑Water and the WHO/UNICEF Joint Monitoring Programme (JMP) for SDG 6.

The tool assesses four core governance domains:

DomainDefinition
Policy Intent (P)Formal policy commitments, legal frameworks, and service standards – what has been promised.
Institutional Capacity (I)Budget allocation, staffing levels, technical capacity, and organisational stability – the ability to deliver.
Execution Reality (E)Physical infrastructure delivery, functionality, and maintenance performance – what is actually built and operating.
Household Experience (H)Affordability, access distance, reliability, water quality, and user satisfaction – the lived reality of the end‑user.

Users score each domain from 1 (very poor) to 5 (excellent). The tool automatically calculates the largest mismatch and provides a pattern‑specific diagnosis and intervention.

Show methodology details (including the friction equation)

Friction is calculated as the sum of squared gaps between each pair of domains: F = (P-I)² + (I-E)² + (E-H)² + (P-H)². Larger gaps are penalised more heavily. The tool does the math for you.

⚙️ What is the Socio-Technical Friction (STF) Framework?

The Socio-Technical Friction (STF) Framework is the theoretical engine behind HydroFric. It was developed through a systematic review of over 130 water governance studies across five Southern African countries – Botswana, Namibia, South Africa, Zambia, and Zimbabwe.

What does "Socio-Technical Friction" mean?
In simple terms, friction is the resistance that slows down or blocks water from reaching households. It happens when the different parts of a water governance system – policies, institutions, infrastructure, and households – are not working together smoothly.

The four domains of the STF Framework:

DomainWhat it asksExample of friction
Policy Intent (P)What did the law promise?A national policy promises water for all, but no budget is allocated.
Institutional Capacity (I)Can the utility deliver?The water office has staff but no vehicles to reach remote villages.
Execution Reality (E)Is the infrastructure actually working?A water point was installed, but it broke and was never repaired.
Household Experience (H)Does the family get clean, affordable water?Households walk long distances daily to fetch water from an unsafe source.

Why "friction" and not just "gap"?
A "gap" is static – it tells you something is missing. Friction is dynamic. It measures how much resistance or energy loss is happening in the system.

What is "Zero-Friction Alignment"?
The ultimate goal of the STF Framework is Zero-Friction Alignment – where P = I = E = H. Success is not about getting high scores. Success is about alignment.

📚 The STF Framework is fully documented in the PhD thesis "Operationalizing Socio-Technical Friction: A Multi-Scalar Pathway to Equitable Rural Water Governance in Semi-Arid Southern Africa" (Musuku, 2026).

👨‍💻 Developer & Partners

HydroFric was created by Godwin Joseph Musuku as part of a PhD research project at the University of Zambia. The tool builds on a systematic review of over 130 studies from five Southern African countries (Botswana, Namibia, South Africa, Zambia, and Zimbabwe). Development is supported by the open‑source community, and the tool is published under a free and open licence.

🔒 Data & Privacy

No data is stored on any server. All scores remain on the user’s own device. Users own their inputs, and the tool requires no login or personal information. Anonymous usage is guaranteed.

🛠️ Technical & Functional Details

The current version (v4.6, April 2026) is in English, with French and Portuguese versions planned. The tool provides real‑time diagnostic outputs, including a friction score, friction level, pattern‑specific analysis, priority intervention, and a gap contribution breakdown.

⚠️ Disclaimer & Limitations

HydroFric is a diagnostic guide, not a substitute for legal, engineering, or expert evaluation. Results depend on the accuracy of the scores entered by the user. The tool is designed for decision support and should always be combined with local knowledge and stakeholder consultation.

📖 How it works (methodology)
The Friction Equation (simplified):
Friction is measured by comparing four domains: Policy Intent (P), Institutional Capacity (I), Execution Reality (E), and Household Experience (H).
Higher scores mean better performance. The larger the gap between domains, the higher the friction.
F = (P-I)² + (I-E)² + (E-H)² + (P-H)²
Scores: 1=Very Poor, 2=Poor, 3=Moderate, 4=Good, 5=Excellent.
